Possums on your roof?

Here's an easy way to keep possums off your roof if they're getting onto it via a cable like your power line.

There are expensive things you can buy, like a possum disk

(not allowed by some power companies because they exert forces on the power cable when flexing in the wind). Or you can buy an ultrasonic possum repeller, but they don't work (I tried one â€” useless).

In the end I worked out my own solution

It's a possum deterrent that

does not cost much (~$25 in parts)
does not exert force or damaging friction on the power cable (or any cable)
is durable
works

All you need is a 1m long piece of K-Flex 9 x 25mm x 1m Fire Rated Elastomeric Pipe Insulation (http://www.bunnings.com.au/k-flex-9-x-32mm-x-1m-fire-rated-elastomeric-pipe-insulation-_p4920092) from Bunnings, a packet of stainless steel nails http://www.bunnings.com.au/paslode-25-x-2-0mm-250g-stainless-steel-fibre-cement-nails-330-pack_p2360521 (they won't rust) and 4 longish cable ties http://www.bunnings.com.au/crescent-300-x-4-8mm-black-cable-ties-25-pack_p4431201 .

Cut off about a 370mm piece of insulation to use, then cut it lengthwise with scissors to allow it to go over the cable. Now push the nails through from the inside of the foam outwards, trying to get as many in as you can. I used about 70% of that packet. Leave a little unused patch of foam at the beginning and end for your cable ties to tie it to the power line.

Slip it over the cable and secure with a cable tie on each end, and two even spaced in the middle to stop it bulging open and allowing the possum to insert a paw there.

So far, 3 months and we have not had a possum on the roof!

I was in no danger at all by installing this soft cuff around a well insulated cable. Do not put your hands anywhere on the cable or posts, get someone to hold the base of the ladder so you can use 2 hands. I never actually touched the cable, and the only thing now resting on the cable is the very soft foam tube.

It was worth it because the darned possums chew the cables on my solar panels and urinate/defecate onto the roof, which feeds into my water tank.

Some people say it's dangerous to do this, so that's why you should get your electrician to attach this to the mains cable, just in case you fall of the high ladder or the cable is in poor condtions and has cracks in the insulation that could be a danger to you. The insulation on the cable is several mm thick, so unless it has perished in the sun, the electrician will be safe. Danger of shock: infinitesimal. The biggest danger is being high up on a ladder.
